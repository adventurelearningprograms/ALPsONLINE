The genetically engineered crop that I have selected is the increased biomass corn produced by Monsanto; the event number is MON 87403. With increased biomass, more corn can be consumed per ear of corn. There is a vast range of applications for maize, all of which can benefit from the trait of increased biomass. Among these applications include human consumption, animal feed, ethanol production, and oil. Monsanto also believes that increasing yield per unit area as opposed to increasing the area itself is the best way to respond to the increasing need for food. More corn produced will also mean more corn can be sold, thus raising the revenue produced by the farming and agriculture sector. Due to the miniscule change to the maize genome, the overall composition of the maize will remain untouched. Thus all the safety of maize is preserved and the environment will not be put under the stress of a new crop that can potentially bolster current pests and weeds. This small change to the maize genome can make a monumental impact for the entire population by producing more food and trade revenue at a low environmental cost.
